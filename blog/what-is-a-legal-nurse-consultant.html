What is a Legal Nurse Consultant?

June 09, 2022

Since the 1980s, Legal Nurse Consultants have been collaborative strategists, supporting their legal partners and clients in navigating healthcare-related cases. Once in a while the big question pops up: what are Legal Nurse Consultants?


Who are these interpreters navigating the legal and medical worlds? This article will break down those questions and give the Legal Nurse Consultant Role a little more color.

What is Legal Nurse Consulting?

Before diving into who a legal nurse consultant is, let’s take a step back and understand what Legal Nurse Consulting is about. At its core, Legal Nurse Consulting is the analysis and evaluation of facts, and the rendering of informed expert opinions related to the delivery of healthcare services and outcomes.


The main role of a Legal Nurse Consultant (LNC) is to help law professionals understand and process medicolegal information. The LNC works to help bridge the infamous gap between medicine and the law. The upswing of an LNC is that they are experts in practical medicine, and have a firm grasp on the legal aspects of the healthcare industry.

What (or who) is a Legal Nurse Consultant?

The American Association of Legal Nurse Consultants summarizes it best: “A Legal Nurse Consultant (LNC) is a licensed registered nurse who performs a critical analysis of clinically related issues in various settings, in the legal arena.”


As a medical insider, who knows the inner workings of the complicated healthcare system, the LNC can assist attorneys and law professionals as they navigate their medically focused cases.


The LNC uses their experience in healthcare to help attorney clients understand various regulations, protocols, and practice standards so the attorney and their team will have a better understanding of healthcare obligations in scenarios related to their case.

What does a Legal Nurse Consultant Do?

The specifics of a Legal Nurse Consultant’s duties will vary based on the case they are consulting on, the needs of the attorney client, or an LNC’s niche (aka practice specialty). Although offerings vary, the services of an LNC include, but are not limited to:


  • Taking part in client interviews
  • Analyze medical records (paper and EMR)
  • Conduct research
  • Define standards of care in medical malpractice cases
  • Evaluate strengths and weaknesses of cases
  • Write or interpret medical language in legal documents
  • Educate lawyers and clients about medical affairs
  • Determine plaintiffs’ future medical needs and costs for care
  • Participate in medical exams
  • Testify as an expert or Subject Matter Expert (SME)
  • Help attorneys prepare for depositions and trials

Developing a partnership with a trusted LNC allows the attorney and/or legal team to have guidance and an overall understanding of the healthcare components of their case.
