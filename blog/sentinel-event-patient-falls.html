Sentinel Event: Patient Falls

July 05, 2022

The Joint Commission on Accreditation of Healthcare Organizations ("The Joint Commission" or JCAHO) defines a Sentinel Event as an unexpected occurrence involving death or serious physical or psychological injury. In January 2021, JACHO amended the definition of falls to assist healthcare institutions in aligning policies and determining if a fall is reportable as a sentinel event. This article will review the policy addendum and how collaborating with a legal nurse consultant will elevate your fall case.

What is a Sentinel Event?

If you are just joining in on our Sentinel Series, you may be wondering what exactly is a Sentinel Event?


A Sentinel Event is a patient safety event not primarily related to the natural course of the patient's illness or underlying condition that results in death, permanent harm, severe harm, temporary harm and/or requires intervention to sustain life.


Each year the Joint Commission publishes a report on the most reported sentinel events of the previous year. January 1, 2021, JACHO's Sentinel Summary Report and Policy was amended to define fall events further. The revision allows for scrutiny into whether a fall is considered a reportable event or not.

Patient Falls - Most Reported Sentinel Event

Patient falls resulting in injury consistently are among the most frequently reviewed Sentinel Events by The Joint Commission. In 2021, patient falls remained the most frequently reported Sentinel Event.


The Sentinel Event Policy's new definition aligns with guidelines from the National Database of Nursing Quality Indicators™ (NDNQI). The revision helps staff in all healthcare settings understand whether a fall should be reviewed as a Sentinel Event. In addition, this revision and definition inclusion will help further expand the knowledge around falls and increase the effectiveness of implementing fall preventive measures. The policy defines a fall as a reportable Sentinel Event as:



“[a] fall resulting in any of the following: any fracture; surgery, casting, or traction; required consult/management or comfort care for a neurological (e.g., skull fracture, subdural or intracranial hemorrhage) or internal (e.g., rib fracture, small liver laceration) injury; a patient with coagulopathy who receives blood products as a result of the fall; or death or permanent harm as a result of injuries sustained from the fall (not from physiologic events causing the fall)”

Legal Nurse Consultant Role In a Falls Case

A Legal Nurse Consultant (LNC), as we have covered before, is an individual with specialized, real-world medical experience as a Registered Nurse combined with an understanding of the legal implications of a case.


In a case involving a fall, the Legal Nurse Consultant combs the plaintiff's records and creates a chronology from before and after the fall, including a Sentinel Event report, if appropriate. The purpose of the review of prior medical care is to determine the baseline or condition of the patient before the fall. Some key facts the LNC will look for include:


  • Was the patient prone to falls?

  • Had the patient had a prior fall causing injury?

  • What were the risk factors that contributed to the fall?

  • Are there any pertinent medical records that are missing?

The attorney can use a chronology to fully understand the patient's pre-existing conditions, injuries, and consequences surrounding the case.


The LNC can also perform medical literature research to see how current literature and practice standard supports or refutes the causation claim. Medical expert recruitment and preparation, medical record summaries, and testimony as experts are some other ways a Legal Nurse Consultant can assist with a falls case.
