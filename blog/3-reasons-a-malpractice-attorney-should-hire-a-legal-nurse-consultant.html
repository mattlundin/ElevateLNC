3 Reasons a Malpractice Attorney Should Hire A Legal Nurse Consultant

Understanding the medical elements and nuances surrounding a malpractice case is crucial for a malpractice attorney or team member. A surefire way to ensure you and your team are prepared and knowledgeable to succeed in the courtroom is with a Legal Nurse Consultant supporting your efforts. The most successful malpractice attorneys utilize the services of an LNC through the lifecycle of their malpractice cases. Below are three reasons you need to partner with an LNC on your next malpractice case.

Invaluable Expertise

A Legal Nurse Consultant is an expert in the healthcare field. They are Registered Nurses who can translate the nuanced medical jargon into layman's terms. The LNC can evaluate the integrity of medical records and analyze the documents for validity, saving the attorney and the team time and averting headaches. The Legal Nurse Consultant's report can provide organized evidence and arguments to speed document preparation and facilitate faster settlements.

Cost-Effective

The LNC can save a malpractice firm time and money through their services. Merit reviews, EMR (medical record) analysis, and medical chart evaluation are a few critical services any plaintiff or defense attorney can benefit from. The Legal Nurse Consultant can analyze and evaluate a medical record to determine completion and validity, which is crucial during case preparation. The LNC can then take the record, organize it, and translate it into a presentation the attorney or legal professional can utilize.


Complicated cases may require research, a skill where the LNC is efficient and effective. Save time droning through files and research with an LNC. They can effectively navigate best practice standards and evidence to collate case support.

Vital Resource

LNCs are well versed in knowing, understanding, and communicating the standard of care. When partnering with a Legal Nurse Consultant, they can walk through whether the case adheres to or upholds the standard of care and again determine the validity of a case.


The Legal Nurse Consultant can help secure a medical expert for your case. (Yes, you may need to invoke a healthcare professional other than a doctor for your case, and the LNC can help determine the best support for the case at hand. The LNC themself can also serve as expert witnesses for your case. Nurses are experts at breaking down complex topics into digestible understood concepts. The LNC would be a great asset to the expert witness panel, especially if the jury requires additional support in understanding complex topics to render a decision.

Bonus – Merit Reviews

Okay, so merit reviews technically are not a bonus considering we discussed this LNC service, but to harness the value of a Merit Review performed by an LNC bears repeating. A thorough merit review serves both plaintiffs and defense attorneys. The Legal Nurse Consultant merit review is the gateway to determining if a case is worth retaining and investing in.
