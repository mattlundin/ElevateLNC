Common Misconceptions about Legal Nurse Consultants

By: Bridget Carey MSN,RN,CWCN



A nurse's skill set can transfer and translate into almost any profession. The law and medical space are a common intersection where nurses contribute their skills and expertise. As you may already know, there is a dedicated specialty and group of nurses working alongside attorneys as Legal Nurse Consultants (LNC). Since the 1980's legal nurse consultants have been working alongside attorneys and legal professionals, guiding their medically focused cases. Sometimes, the lines and limitations of the Legal Nurse Consultant's role blur. This article explores some of the misconceptions about the LNC role.

Legal Nurse Consultant vs. Paralegal

Paralegals and Legal Nurse Consultants (LNC) are two critical team members in any medicolegal case. (Dare we say partners in crime?) Although the two are important stakeholders on medically focused cases, and some responsibilities overlap, it is important to understand the difference in their roles.


The LNC is a Registered Nurse who uses their nursing and healthcare expertise to assist the attorney and legal professionals in educating their team about medical events in their cases. The LNC's responsibilities include analyzing medical records, interpreting medical language, conducting research, and helping attorneys prepare for depositions.


Paralegals are legal professionals who work under the supervision of an attorney. Most paralegals have a degree or professional certificate in paralegal studies from an accredited program approved by the American Bar Association. The paralegal's responsibilities include conducting legal research, drafting legal documents, and assisting with discovery and pleadings.


As an LNC, it is important to have a working relationship with the paralegals within the firm or team you are working with. It is also important for the paralegal to understand the Legal Nurse Consultant's role.

Legal Nurse Consultant vs. Subject Matter Expert (SME)

According to the US Office of Personnel Management (OPM) a subject matter expert, or SME, is a "person with bona fide expert knowledge about what it takes to do a particular job."


In essence, a Legal Nurse Consultant is an SME in healthcare matters, and even more so within the specialty they practice. However, when it comes to being a member of a team working on a medical case, the LNC role and SME role can stand on their own.


Establishing role expectations will be critical when reviewing the needs of the medicolegal case. A Legal Nurse Consultant can participate as a Subject Matter Expert, or the LNC can interview and recruit SMEs on behalf of the attorney-client.

Legal Nurse Consultant vs. Expert Witness

Per the Academy of Experts, an expert witness is an individual with knowledge or experience within a discipline beyond that of a layperson. An expert witness's duty and primary function are to provide an impartial, expert opinion on particulars of a case based on the information provided.


You are correct in your assessment if you’re mentally ticking off boxes where the LNC overlaps with this definition. A Legal Nurse Consultant meets the criteria and can testify as an expert witness for a case. The LNC can also research, interview, and prepare an expert witness to best support the medical case. The LNC’s expertise is up-leveled, where they can analyze the case at hand and identify where an expert witness would benefit the plaintiff or defense attorney.
